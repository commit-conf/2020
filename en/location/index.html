Where

Address

We are at San Pablo CEU University, Montepríncipe Campus. Boadilla del monte, Madrid · Open in Google Maps.

How to get there

You can use public transport, taxi, car, or any of the free shuttles that the organization will provide during the event. Because the parking space is limited, we recommend not to go by car and to use public transport, which is surprisingly simple.

Metro L3 (Montepríncipe)
Bus 571 · Bus 573

Free shuttles

We provide a free shuttle service to/from Plaza de España that takes 20 minutes to do a one-way trip. We have adjusted the schedule according to our experience, but we cannot guarantee seats for everyone in every shuttle. We will publish the timetable and more details on our blog.

These shuttles are provided as a courtesy, and may not have enough capacity for everyone.

Will you have a cloakroom?

We will have storage space for your coat, helmet, or suitcase. The cloakroom will always have someone from the organization or volunteers. However, neither Commit or the University will be responsible for your belongings.

Badges

We are going to be strict with access to the talks. With this in mind, at the registration desk, we will give you:

  • A wristband: This wristband must be showed when asked by the organization at the doors of the tracks. It will also be necessary to pick your lunch box, and you must wear it during the entire event. Neither in the pocket, nor the backpack, nor at home. Without a wristband, the organization reserves the right to prohibit access to the talks.

    If you want to use the same ticket for one person the first day and another one the second, tell it at the info point before leaving the event, and we will cut your wristband and give you another for the next day.

  • A badge: The only purpose of it is to facilitate networking. It is not mandatory to wear it, but it will help to meet new people.

Registration and access control

We will use the Koliseo Access Control app to verify your ticket. You only have to bring your ticket with you and wear the wristband we will give you.

You can bring your ticket either printed out or on your mobile device.

I will not be able to make it to the first session on the first day, or I will only go on Saturday. Will I be able to register?

The agenda specifies the official registration period, but you can register anytime, either Friday or Saturday, at the Info Point in the main entrance.

Once you registered for Friday, you do not need to register again for Saturday, just wear your wristband and the badge we gave you since there will be people controlling.

Do I have to bring my laptop?

If you want to go to a workshop, you will most probably need it. It could also be useful to take notes or review the speaker slides during the talk.

Please mind your laptop battery life. There will be power plugs in lab tracks, but not for talks.

What do I need to attend a workshop?

Each speaker will specify their workshop requirements on the agenda: laptop, install a specific application, previous experience, etc.

Do I need to register to attend a talk?

You do not need to register for talks. Your ticket allows you to attend any talk or lab. Although we distribute them through the tracks the best way possible, some of them will be more crowded than others, and you will not be able to get a seat for some specific one.

To help us understand the talks with the bigger expected audience, you can specify the talks you are more interested in on the agenda page. We will assign the biggest rooms to the talks with the bigger expected audience. Here you have a tweet with instructions on how to do this.
